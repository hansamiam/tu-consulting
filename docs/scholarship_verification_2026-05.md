## 1. Chevening Scholarships (UK, FCDO)

- **Official URL:** https://www.chevening.org/scholarship/
- **Application window (next intake, 2027/28 cycle):** Opens **August 2026**, closes **7 October 2026, 12:00 UTC**. The 2026/27 cycle closed on 7 October 2025 — currently between cycles. Successful candidates must hold at least one unconditional UK university offer by **17:00 BST, 9 July 2027** (deadline pattern repeats).
- **Eligibility:**
  - Citizen of a Chevening-eligible country/territory — **Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan are both eligible** (dedicated country pages exist at `/scholarship/kazakhstan/` and `/scholarship/kyrgyzstan/`).
  - Undergraduate degree that qualifies for a UK master's.
  - **At least 2,800 hours of work experience** post-undergrad (~2 years FT, can be paid, voluntary, internships).
  - Apply to **3 different eligible UK master's courses**.
  - Commit to returning home for **at least 2 years** after the award.
  - English: meet the language requirement of your chosen UK university (typically IELTS 6.5–7.0). Chevening itself does not impose a separate IELTS minimum at application — only at university-offer stage.
  - No British/dual-British citizenship.
- **Award value:** Full tuition (capped at **£22,000 for MBA**, uncapped otherwise) + monthly stipend of **£1,690 (London)** or **£1,378 (outside London)** + economy return airfare + visa fees + arrival/departure allowances + thesis grant. Total typically **£35k–£50k**.
- **Application requirements:** 4 essays (Leadership, Networking, Studying in UK, Career Plan, ~500 words each); 2 references (uploaded only after conditional selection); UK university admission (one unconditional offer required by July deadline); transcript; passport.
- **Number of awards / selectivity:** ~1,500 globally per cycle. Country-level intake for KZ/KG is single digits — country pages do not publish exact quotas.
- **Confidence:** Verified from chevening.org and `/scholarships/application-timeline/` on 2026-05-09. Note: 2027/28 cycle has not yet opened — dates above match Chevening's published recurring annual pattern (open August, close 7 Oct), to be reconfirmed once their 2027/28 timeline page goes live in summer 2026.

## 2. Fulbright Foreign Student Program (US, IIE) — KZ + KG

- **Official URL (program):** https://foreign.fulbrightonline.org/
- **Country-specific URLs:** https://kz.usembassy.gov/fulbright-foreign-student-program/ (KZ); https://kg.usembassy.gov/fulbright-foreign-student-program-in-central-asia/ (KG)
- **Application window (next intake, 2027–2028 cycle, currently OPEN):**
  - **Kazakhstan:** deadline **15 July 2026, 23:59 Astana time**.
  - **Kyrgyzstan:** deadline **5 June 2026, 23:59 local time**.
  - Online portal: https://apply.iie.org/ffsp2027
  - Grantees travel to US July/August 2027.
- **Eligibility:**
  - Citizen of and resident in KZ or KG at time of application.
  - Hold a completed bachelor's degree (US equivalent) before grant start.
  - **At least 2 years of substantive professional work experience** post-undergrad.
  - Master's-level study (PhD is generally not funded under FFSP for KZ/KG; check country page).
  - English proficiency required — TOEFL submitted as part of application; IIE arranges institutional TOEFL for shortlisted candidates. Competitive scores typically **TOEFL iBT 90+ / IELTS 6.5+**.
  - Priority fields: STEM, business, agriculture (KZ).
  - Cannot hold US citizenship or permanent residency.
- **Award value:** Full tuition + fees + monthly maintenance stipend (varies by host city) + round-trip international airfare + ASPE health insurance + book/settling-in allowance + limited dependent allowance (rare). Typical total value $40k–$80k+/year depending on placement.
- **Application requirements:** Online IIE application (Slate/Embark), study objective essay, personal statement, 3 references, transcripts, English test, CV, passport bio page. Embassy-level interview for shortlisted candidates.
- **Number of awards / selectivity:** Not publicly published per country; typically **~3–8 awards/year for KZ**, **~2–5/year for KG**. Highly selective.
- **Confidence:** Verified from kz.usembassy.gov (cached PDF: Instructions-Foreign_Student_Program-Online-Application-IIE-Placed_26-27.pdf) and kg.usembassy.gov on 2026-05-09. **Note:** the live `kz.usembassy.gov/fulbright-foreign-student-program/` page returned "Technical Difficulties" on direct fetch on 2026-05-09 — deadline confirmed via embassy PDF and search snippets; reconfirm before publishing.

## 3. MEXT Scholarship (Japan) — Embassy-Recommended track, KZ + KG

- **Official URL:** https://www.studyinjapan.go.jp/en/smap-stopj-applications-research.html (program); country deadlines published by Embassy of Japan in Kazakhstan (kz.emb-japan.go.jp) and Embassy of Japan in the Kyrgyz Republic (kg.emb-japan.go.jp).
- **Application window (next intake, 2027 arrival):** Embassy recruitment runs **April–May 2026** (currently OPEN). Country-specific deadlines vary by embassy; analogous embassies posted deadlines of **21 May–5 June 2026** for the 2027 cycle. **KZ and KG embassy-specific deadlines could not be verified from the official embassy sites on 2026-05-09 — needs manual check before launch.**
- **Eligibility:**
  - Citizen of a country with diplomatic relations with Japan — **KZ and KG both eligible**.
  - Cannot hold Japanese citizenship.
  - Age limits: **under 25** (Undergraduate / Specialized Training / College of Tech), **under 35** (Research Student / Master's / PhD), **under 35** (Teacher Training, with 5+ years teaching).
  - GPA roughly 2.30/3.00 (varies by category).
  - Health requirements + willingness to learn Japanese.
  - Language: must pass embassy-administered written exams (English + Japanese for most categories; +math/science for undergraduate).
- **Award value:**
  - Monthly stipend: **¥117,000** (Teacher Training), **¥143,000–¥145,000** (Research Student / Master's / PhD), **¥117,000** (Undergraduate — note: studyinjapan.go.jp page also lists ¥242,000 figure, which appears to be an error or different category; treat ¥117k–¥145k as the verified working range and reconfirm).
  - Tuition: **fully exempted** at the host university.
  - Travel: round-trip economy airfare provided.
  - Visa fees waived; no health insurance directly funded (scholars typically enroll in Japan's National Health Insurance).
- **Application requirements:** Application form, field-of-study and research-plan documents, transcripts, graduation certificate, recommendation letter, medical certificate, photos. Written exams (English + Japanese), then interview at the embassy. Two-stage screening: embassy primary screening (May–Aug 2026), MEXT final screening (winter 2026/27).
- **Number of awards / selectivity:** Globally ~3,000+ MEXT recipients/year across all categories. Country quotas not published; KZ and KG typically **<10 placements/year** combined.
- **Confidence:** Program-level details verified from studyinjapan.go.jp on 2026-05-09. Country-specific deadlines for KZ and KG **unverified — needs manual check on kz.emb-japan.go.jp / kg.emb-japan.go.jp before launch.** Stipend figure for Undergraduate has a discrepancy on the official page — flag for manual reconfirmation.

## 4. Mastercard Foundation Scholars Program

- **Official URL:** https://mastercardfdn.org/en/what-we-do/our-programs/mastercard-foundation-scholars-program/
- **CRITICAL FINDING — NOT ELIGIBLE FOR CENTRAL ASIA:** The Mastercard Foundation Scholars Program is exclusively for **citizens of Sub-Saharan African countries** (and a small number of displaced/refugee youth in specific contexts). All partner universities (U of Toronto, Cambridge, ASU, Makerere, Pretoria, UBC, etc.) explicitly require applicants to be **citizens of and currently residing in a Sub-Saharan African country**.
- **Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan citizens are NOT eligible.** This program should be **removed from the database** for Central Asian users, or filtered out of recommendation results. Listing it risks user trust and wastes user effort.
- **Confidence:** Verified from mastercardfdn.org and University of Toronto MCF Scholars announcement (internationalexperience.utoronto.ca) on 2026-05-09. No partner institution lists Central Asia in scope.

## 5. Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarships

- **Official URL:** https://vanier.gc.ca/en/home-accueil.html
- **CRITICAL FINDING — PROGRAM DISCONTINUED:** The Vanier CGS program **closed permanently** after the Fall 2024 competition (results released April 2025). It has been **replaced by the Canada Graduate Research Scholarship – Doctoral (CGRS-D)** under a new harmonized Tri-Agency (CIHR/NSERC/SSHRC) program.
- **Replacement (CGRS-D) summary:**
  - Value: **CAD $40,000/year for up to 3 years** (down from Vanier's $50k/year).
  - Open to Canadian citizens, PRs, and international students nominated by a Canadian institution.
  - Open to first doctoral degree applicants.
  - Application deadlines and process administered by individual Canadian universities — applicants do not apply directly to the federal program.
- **Action required:** Vanier listing in the Top Uni database must be either **removed or relabeled "Discontinued — see CGRS-D"** with a redirect to a CGRS-D entry. Publishing it as live will burn user trust immediately.
- **Confidence:** Verified from vanier.gc.ca, sgs.utoronto.ca/awards/vanier-canada-graduate-scholarship/, and ualberta.ca grad studies pages on 2026-05-09.
